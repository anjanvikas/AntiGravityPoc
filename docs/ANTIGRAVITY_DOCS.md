Antigravity creates special files to keep your project organized. Think of them as your project management tools.

### 1. `task.md` (The Checklist)
*   **What is it?**: A simple list of what needs to be done.
*   **Why it's useful**: Keeps you on track. You always know what's finished and what's next.
*   **Enterprise Value**: Managers love this. They can see progress instantly without interrupting you.

### 2. `implementation_plan.md` (The Blueprint)
*   **What is it?**: A detailed plan written *before* any code is touched.
*   **Why it's useful**: "Measure twice, cut once." It prevents messy code by planning the architecture first.
*   **Enterprise Value**: Senior engineers can review the plan and catch design errors early, saving weeks of wasted work.

### 3. `walkthrough.md` (The Proof)
*   **What is it?**: A report showing what was built, including screenshots and test results.
*   **Why it's useful**: Proof that it works. You don't have to manually demonstrate it to everyone.
*   **Enterprise Value**: QA teams and Product Managers can see exactly what changed, speeding up approvals.
